Product Walkthrough

From request to completed ride ‚Äî end to end.

Here is how a complete trip flows across all three sides of the platform simultaneously.

1
Rider App

Rider Opens App & Enters Destination

The rider opens Halla Taxi, sees their live location on a Mombasa map, and types or selects a destination. Popular spots (Nyali, Likoni Ferry, JKIA connector, Diani) are pre-suggested. The app shows fare estimate and ETA before confirming.

2
Rider App

Rider Selects Ride Tier & Confirms with M-Pesa

Three tiers are shown ‚Äî Economy, Comfort, XL ‚Äî with live pricing in KES. Rider confirms and an M-Pesa STK Push is sent to their phone. Payment is captured before the driver is dispatched.

3
Driver App

Nearest Driver Receives Trip Request

The system finds the closest available, verified driver. A full-screen alert appears on their app with pickup location, estimated fare, and a 15-second countdown to accept or decline. If declined, the next driver is tried automatically.

4
Rider App

Rider Tracks Driver Live

Once matched, the rider sees the driver's car moving on the map in real time. Driver name, photo, car details (make, color, Kenyan plate), and star rating are all visible. One-tap calling and messaging are available.

5
Driver App

Driver Navigates to Pickup, then Destination

The driver app provides turn-by-turn navigation first to the rider, then to the destination. The fare meter runs live during the trip. An SOS button is accessible to both parties throughout.

6
Admin Dashboard

Admin Monitors Everything in Real Time

Throughout the trip, the Halla Taxi admin dashboard shows every active trip on a live map, with the ability to intervene, contact either party, or flag issues. All trip data is logged automatically.

7
Rider App

Trip Completed ‚Äî Rating & M-Pesa Confirmation

Rider receives an M-Pesa confirmation SMS and sees a trip summary in-app. They rate the driver. The driver's earning is logged. Halla Taxi commission is automatically deducted. Both parties receive receipts.

Risk Mitigation

We've thought about what can go wrong.

Every project has risks. We name them honestly and tell you exactly how we handle them ‚Äî because surprises are worse than problems.

Timeline Delays Medium Risk

Complex integrations (M-Pesa callbacks, real-time WebSockets) can take longer than estimated, particularly during Safaricom's sandbox approval process.

Mitigation: Buffer weeks built into every phase. M-Pesa integration begins in Phase 1 specifically to surface issues early. Weekly client progress updates ensure no surprises.

Scope Creep Medium Risk

As the product comes alive, new feature requests will naturally emerge. Without discipline, this can derail timelines and budgets silently.

Mitigation: A signed scope document defines MVP features before development starts. New requests are logged, costed, and added to a Phase 2 backlog ‚Äî not inserted mid-build.

Post-Launch Bugs Managed

No software launches perfectly. Real users will find edge cases that testing didn't catch ‚Äî payment timeouts, location edge cases, device-specific issues.

Mitigation: 30-day post-launch support is included in Phase 4 at no extra cost. A staging environment mirrors production for safe testing before every release.

Driver Adoption Medium Risk

Technology means nothing without drivers on the platform. A great app with no drivers is unusable for riders.

Mitigation: Our Business Lead manages a parallel driver recruitment and onboarding program during development ‚Äî so drivers are ready at launch, not after it.

Third-Party API Changes Low Risk

M-Pesa Daraja, Google Maps, and Africa's Talking APIs can change pricing or deprecate features with limited notice.

Mitigation: All integrations are built behind abstraction layers ‚Äî swapping an API provider requires changes in one place, not throughout the codebase.

Competitive Response Low Risk

Bolt or Little Cab could increase focus on Mombasa in response to a strong local competitor entering the market.

Mitigation: Halla Taxi's advantage is local specificity ‚Äî Swahili UX, Mombasa-native pricing, community trust, and a business owner who understands this market. That cannot be copied quickly from Nairobi.
